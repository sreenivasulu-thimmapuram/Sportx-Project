Physical health

Physical health refers to the well-being and proper functioning of the body and its systems. It is achieved through regular physical activity, a balanced diet, adequate rest, and avoiding harmful habits like smoking or excessive alcohol consumption. Physical health includes maintaining fitness, strength, flexibility, and cardiovascular endurance, and involves the absence of disease, injury, or chronic pain, allowing the body to function optimally in daily activities.

1.Heart Disease

Risk factors: High blood pressure, high cholesterol, smoking, obesity

Prevention: egular aerobic exercise (150 minutes/week), strength training, healthy diet

Symptoms: Chest pain, shortness of breath, fatigue

2. Diabetes

Risk factors: Obesity, physical inactivity, family history

Prevention: Regular aerobic exercise (150 minutes/week), strength training, healthy diet, weight management

Symptoms: Increased thirst, urination, fatigue, blurred vision

3.Obesity

Risk factors: Poor diet, sedentary lifestyle, genetics

Symptoms: Weight gain, body fat increase, decreased mobility

Prevention: Healthy diet, regular exercise (aerobic and strength training), portion control

4.Osteoporosis

Risk factors: : Sedentary lifestyle, low calcium intake, family history

Symptoms: Weak bones, fractures, loss of height

Prevention: Regular aerobic exercise (150 minutes/week), strength training, healthy diet

5.Certain Cancers (e.g., Colon, Breast, Prostate)

Risk factors: Sedentary lifestyle, low calcium intake, family history

Prevention: Regular weight-bearing exercise (walking, running, strength training), calcium-rich diet

Symptoms: Sudden weakness, numbness, confusion, vision loss

6.Stroke

Risk factors: High blood pressure, high cholesterol, smoking, physical inactivity

Prevention: Regular aerobic exercise (150 minutes/week), strength training, healthy diet

Symptoms: Symptoms: Sudden weakness, numbness, confusion, vision loss
